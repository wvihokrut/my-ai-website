MEET MACK

Mack Vihokrut is a former Buddhist monk, higher education advocate, and researcher. Raised with a strong foundation in mindfulness, Mack helps students from underrepresented backgrounds find their path.

With experience at Johns Hopkins University and New York University, Mack's expertise includes education, healthcare, and research. Having overcome personal traumas, Mack raises awareness on mentorship, empathy, and mental health services for young people.

Mack is a TEDx speaker, whose viral "How to Save Your Relationship in 5 Minutes?" talk has inspired audiences worldwide. He is also a sought-after author, with his upcoming book "Unreasonable Empathy" exploring radical compassion in education and beyond.

WHY MACK

Do your higher education organizations struggle with disconnection and empathy deficits among students, faculty, and staff? Are these relational challenges hindering academic success, student well-being, and institutional progress?

Mack helps colleges and universities build stronger relationships. He has personal experience showing kindness and empathy to others. Mack learned that when we care deeply about others, amazing things can happen. Now, Mack can help your organization create a more supportive community, where students and staff thrive.

WHAT TO EXPECT

Mack's keynote will take your campus community on a transformative journey of self-discovery, empathy, and connection. Afterward, they'll leave with a renewed sense of purpose and practical strategies to foster deeper relationships and empathy. His message will challenge their perspective on relationships and have them embracing Mack's core principle: empathy is the foundation of human connection. And with the right mindset, moments of conflict become opportunities for growth and understanding.

With Mack leading the charge, your campus community's goal of creating a culture of empathy and compassion is within reach.
